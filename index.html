Arequipa, Perú

Arequipa, known as the "White City" of Peru, is famous for its stunning colonial architecture made from sillar, a white volcanic stone. It is surrounded by breathtaking landscapes and dominated by the majestic Misti Volcano, which rises to 5,822 meters (19,101 feet) above sea level. Misti, an iconic stratovolcano, adds to the city's charm and serves as a popular destination for hikers and adventurers. Arequipa blends history, culture, and natural beauty, making it one of Peru’s most captivating cities.
